# Phase 1: Math & Physics Foundations

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks
**Prerequisites:** Basic Python, high school math
**Capstone:** matrix-lab — Linear algebra toolkit for quantum computing

## Objectives

By the end of this phase, you will:
- Manipulate complex numbers and understand their geometric meaning
- Work fluently with vectors, matrices, and linear transformations
- Compute eigenvalues/eigenvectors and understand their physical significance
- Use Dirac notation (bra-ket) naturally
- Construct tensor products for multi-particle systems
- Understand Hilbert spaces as the stage for quantum mechanics

## Weekly Plan

### Week 1: Complex Numbers & Vector Spaces
- Complex arithmetic, polar form, Euler's formula
- Vector spaces, linear independence, basis, dimension
- Exercises: implement complex number operations, visualize on Argand diagram

### Week 2: Inner Products & Matrices
- Inner products, norms, orthogonality
- Matrix operations, special matrices (diagonal, symmetric, triangular)
- Exercises: Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization, matrix decompositions

### Week 3: Eigentheory & Special Matrices
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
- Unitary matrices (preserve inner products)
- Hermitian matrices (real eigenvalues, orthogonal eigenvectors)
- Exercises: spectral decomposition, verify unitarity

### Week 4: Tensor Products & Dirac Notation
- Kronecker product construction and properties
- Bra-ket notation: kets |ψ⟩, bras ⟨φ|, inner products ⟨φ|ψ⟩
- Outer products |ψ⟩⟨φ|
- Exercises: construct Bell states, compute expectation values

### Week 5: Probability & Information Theory
- Probability distributions, expectation, variance
- Shannon entropy, mutual information
- Classical bits and information measures
- Exercises: entropy calculations, information-theoretic problems

### Week 6: Capstone Project
- Build matrix-lab: reusable linear algebra toolkit
- Include: complex vector operations, matrix classes, Dirac notation helpers
- Test suite with physics-relevant test cases
