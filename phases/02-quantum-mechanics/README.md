# Phase 2: Quantum Mechanics Core

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks
**Prerequisites:** Phase 1 (linear algebra, Dirac notation)
**Capstone:** qm-sandbox — Interactive quantum mechanics simulations

## Objectives

By the end of this phase, you will:
- State and apply the postulates of quantum mechanics
- Simulate time evolution using the Schrodinger equation
- Compute measurement probabilities using the Born rule
- Construct and analyze entangled states
- Prove the no-cloning theorem
- Work with density matrices for mixed states

## Weekly Plan

### Week 1: Postulates of QM
- State vectors in Hilbert space
- Observables as Hermitian operators
- Measurement postulate and Born rule
- Exercises: compute measurement outcomes for spin-1/2

### Week 2: Time Evolution
- Schrodinger equation (time-dependent and time-independent)
- Unitary evolution operators
- Stationary states and energy eigenstates
- Exercises: simulate particle in a box, harmonic oscillator

### Week 3: Superposition & Interference
- Superposition principle with concrete examples
- Interference patterns (double slit, Mach-Zehnder)
- Phase and its physical meaning
- Exercises: simulate interference, visualize probability amplitudes

### Week 4: Entanglement
- Bell states construction and properties
- EPR paradox and Bell's inequality
- Entanglement as a resource
- No-cloning theorem proof
- Exercises: verify Bell inequality violation, construct GHZ states

### Week 5: Density Matrices & Mixed States
- Pure vs mixed states
- Density operator formalism
- Partial trace and reduced density matrices
- Von Neumann entropy
- Exercises: compute purity, trace out subsystems

### Week 6: Capstone Project
- Build qm-sandbox: interactive QM simulations
- Include: state evolution, measurement simulation, entanglement demos
- Visualizations with matplotlib
