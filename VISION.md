# Vision: Quantum Computing & Physics Mastery

## Why

Quantum computing sits at the intersection of physics, mathematics, and computer science — three domains that reward deep understanding over surface familiarity. As a data scientist, I want to build genuine expertise: not just "use Qiskit" but understand *why* quantum algorithms work, *how* quantum mechanics constrains computation, and *where* the frontier problems lie.

## Principles

1. **Code everything** — Every concept gets implemented. No passive reading.
2. **Build intuition before formalism** — Start with 2x2 matrices and concrete examples, then generalize.
3. **Implement before importing** — Build from numpy before using Qiskit. Understand the machinery.
4. **Ship progressively** — Each phase produces a working project, not just notes.
5. **Teach to learn** — If I can't explain it simply, I don't understand it yet.
6. **Connect to real hardware** — Theory without practice is incomplete. Run on actual quantum computers.

## Success Milestones

- [ ] **Explain quantum teleportation** to a non-physicist using only a whiteboard
- [ ] **Build a circuit simulator** from scratch (numpy only, no frameworks)
- [ ] **Run an algorithm on IBM quantum hardware** and interpret the results
- [ ] **Merge an open-source PR** to Qiskit, Cirq, or PennyLane
- [ ] **Reproduce a published paper's** quantum computing results
- [ ] **Publish original research** or a significant open-source contribution

## North Star

Move from consumer of quantum tools to contributor to quantum knowledge. The end state is not "I completed a course" — it's "I can read an arXiv paper, understand the physics, implement the algorithm, run it on hardware, and identify what could be improved."
